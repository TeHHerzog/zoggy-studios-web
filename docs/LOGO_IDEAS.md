# Zoggy Studios Logo Ideas

## Current Direction

The current temporary mark is a bold `Z` in a rounded square with a small spark/orb. It is intentionally simple enough to work for games, apps, writing, and technology projects without looking only like a dark fantasy game logo.

Use it as a starter mark, not a final brand lockup.

## What The Logo Should Communicate

- broad creative studio
- solo-led but professional
- playful without looking childish
- flexible across games, apps, books, scripts, and tools
- memorable at favicon size
- not locked to one genre
- not only dark fantasy

## Concepts To Explore

### 1. The Spark Z

A strong geometric `Z` with a small star, spark, or orb. This suggests ideas becoming projects and works well as an app icon, favicon, or game launcher mark.

Best for:

- current site
- broad creative identity
- tech-friendly applications

Prompt:

```text
Design a clean vector logo for "Zoggy Studios", a solo-led creative and technology studio for games, apps, books, scripts, and strange new worlds. Use a bold geometric letter Z inside a rounded square, with one small spark or orb suggesting imagination and invention. Friendly, memorable, professional, not childish, not dark fantasy only. Flat SVG style, high contrast, works at favicon size, no mockup, no 3D, no gradients unless very subtle.
```

### 2. The Portal Mark

A `Z` shaped like a doorway, portal, or path into a world. This connects games, fiction, scripts, and worldbuilding.

Best for:

- story-heavy projects
- game studio feel
- future books/scripts

Prompt:

```text
Create a vector logo for "Zoggy Studios" where the letter Z forms a stylized portal or doorway into a strange world. The mark should feel imaginative, cinematic, and versatile across games, apps, novels, and scripts. Use simple shapes, strong silhouette, warm light accent, deep ink outline, professional indie studio tone, readable at small sizes, SVG-friendly.
```

### 3. The Workshop Badge

A badge or seal that feels like a maker's workshop: creative tools, systems, code, and worlds.

Best for:

- studio credibility
- app/tool projects
- portfolio pages

Prompt:

```text
Design a modern badge logo for "Zoggy Studios", a creative workshop for games, apps, writing, scripts, and technology experiments. Combine a bold Z monogram with subtle hints of craft, code, and worldbuilding. Avoid literal game controllers, keyboards, books, or film reels. Make it clean, flexible, vector-based, friendly but professional, suitable for a website header and favicon.
```

### 4. The Strange Worlds Wordmark

A custom wordmark where the letters feel slightly unusual but still readable. The mark is less icon-first and more studio-name-first.

Best for:

- homepage hero
- social banners
- future press kit

Prompt:

```text
Create a custom wordmark for "Zoggy Studios" with a friendly, slightly strange creative-tech personality. It should feel suitable for games, apps, books, scripts, and media. Use expressive but readable letterforms, subtle odd angles, no horror styling, no corporate SaaS blandness. Provide a simple black-and-white version and a warm color version. Vector logo design, no background mockup.
```

### 5. The Constellation Z

A `Z` drawn from connected points, implying many projects connected under one studio.

Best for:

- broad umbrella brand
- future multi-project portfolio
- technology + creative blend

Prompt:

```text
Design a vector symbol for "Zoggy Studios" using a constellation-like Z made of connected points and simple geometric strokes. It should suggest many creative projects connected under one studio: games, apps, books, scripts, and technology. Clean, memorable, modern, accessible, strong at small sizes, not too sci-fi, not too fantasy, no complex illustration.
```

## What To Avoid

- generic game controller logos
- dragon-only or sword-only marks
- fake AAA studio polish
- too many symbols at once
- unreadable fantasy lettering
- AI-looking gradients and noisy textures
- copying an existing studio or game logo

## Optional Taste-Skill Workflow

The `leonxlnx/taste-skill` project is useful as an optional design-process reference. Its strongest ideas for this project are:

- use a design system before writing components
- set visual dials for variance, motion, and density
- avoid generic centered-template layouts
- use image-generation prompts for brand boards before locking a logo
- audit existing UI before redesigning it

Do not vendor external skill files into this repository unless explicitly needed. For now, use the ideas as a design checklist and keep project-specific rules in `DESIGN.md`.
